Medical Conditions We Analyze

Addison's Disease

Addison's disease, also known as primary adrenal insufficiency, is a rare disorder that occurs when your adrenal glands don't produce enough of certain hormones, including cortisol and often aldosterone.

Key Symptoms:

  • Extreme fatigue
  • Weight loss
  • Darkening of skin
  • Low blood pressure

Diagnosis:

Blood tests for cortisol and ACTH levels, ACTH stimulation test, and imaging of adrenal glands.

Treatment Overview

Hormone Replacement
Symptom Management
Emergency Preparedness

Treatment Options:

  • Hydrocortisone or prednisone
  • Fludrocortisone for aldosterone
  • Increased salt intake
  • Emergency hydrocortisone injection

Did You Know?

Addison's disease affects about 1 in 100,000 people. President John F. Kennedy had Addison's disease.
